Overview

A full-stack analytics workflow on Oracle

This project demonstrates an end-to-end retail analytics pipeline built on Oracle AI Data Platform Workbench and Oracle Analytics Cloud. Starting from a raw 10,000-row transaction dataset, the workflow covers ingestion, exploratory analysis, feature engineering, predictive modelling, and interactive dashboarding.

The goal was to quantify what drives revenue performance and give business stakeholders a live, self-serve view of profitability, category performance, and customer segment behaviour within the Oracle ecosystem.

Key Findings

What the data revealed

$2.43M
Electronics and Beauty Revenue

Both categories led total revenue at $2.43M each, with Clothing close behind at $2.33M. This indicates a well-balanced, diversified product portfolio with no over-reliance on any single category.

-$31
Revenue Impact per 1% Discount

Each percentage point increase in discount rate reduces predicted revenue by approximately $31. This quantifies the real cost of discounting and gives the business a lever to optimise promotional strategy.

1/3
Each Customer Segment Share

Consumer (32.9%), Corporate (33.3%), and Home Office (33.8%) each hold virtually equal shares of profit margin. A remarkably balanced customer base with no single segment dominating performance.

Nov
Weakest Revenue Month

November 2024 was the softest month at approximately $920K, bucking typical retail seasonality. Revenue remained consistent throughout the year, ranging from $920K to $1.05M, showing a stable and resilient business.

Predictive Model

Linear regression on revenue drivers

Using scikit-learn, a linear regression model was trained on two features, Discount Rate and Cost, to predict Revenue. The model achieved an R squared of 0.957, explaining over 95% of revenue variance in the test set.

from sklearn.model_selection import train_test_split
from sklearn.linear_model import LinearRegression

features = df[['Discount_Rate', 'Cost']]
target = df['Revenue']

X_train, X_test, y_train, y_test = train_test_split(
    features, target,
    test_size=0.2, random_state=42
)

model = LinearRegression()
model.fit(X_train, y_train)
